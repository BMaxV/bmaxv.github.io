critical communication failure.

If you have been linked here, there is a problem. Someone did not get the information that is necessary for something to happen. Or time was wasted on a non issue.

It might concern me, it might concern someone else and I am interested in the outcome. Or someone else found this applicable.

0. ideal situation

The ideal situation goes like this:

  • 0.1 there is a problem
  • 0.2 information about the problem is being shared
  • 0.3 people can consume the information
  • 0.4 draw conclusions
  • 0.5 and start acting to solve the problem.

Any step of this process can fail. This has happened.

1. failure analysis

The way I approach Information I find is this:

1.1 relevance

The piece is irrelevant and wasting my time unless:

  • 1.1.1 it concerns me personally
  • 1.1.2 I can do something about the subject
  • 1.1.3 it concerns my idealism of justice or correctness and only when there is a major new development.

1.2. format

I expect the piece of the information to take my use of the information into account, when the information is being compiled. The piece should be tailored in such a way that I can easily find answers to my questions and reconstruct the ideas the author meant to imply.

I expect the piece to be

  • 1.2.1 succinct
  • 1.2.2 indexed
  • 1.2.3 provide links and references

1.3.consequence

  • 1.3.1 if its a call to action I expect
    • 1.3.1.1 actionable information and instructions
    • 1.3.1.2 or a point of contact
  • 1.3.2 if its about an argument, I assume the author wants to convince me or for me to convince someone else using the piece as a resource.

    To do this need the information to be

  • 1.3.2.1 reasonably complete
  • 1.3.2.2 logically correct
  • 1.3.2.3 grammatically correct and unambiguous
  • 1.3.2.4 detail common ground
  • 1.3.2.5 be "convincing"* or well sourced

*convincing is circular here, but it's a catch all phrase that makes sense in context.

3. solution and prevention

This kind of problem occurs everywhere and all the time I would like to take more action but I can not.

3.1 Quality Control Feedbackloop

The quality of information can be controlled like any other variable. This is not done enough. The way to do it is this:

Each part of the graphic should have a responsible person who can be contacted asked to provide confirmation or details.

It should also be possible for any stakeholder to confirm any detail of any part of this at any time.

Repetition speed of the feedback loop is critical. Faster is better.

3.2 Planning

For the action part, a plan is required. A plan must contain:

  • 3.2.1.The objective
  • 3.2.2 Who is taking action
  • 3.2.3 What is to be done
  • 3.2.4 How much time is needed
  • 3.2.5 What the budget is

This information has to be as explicit and precise as possible.

3.3 Delegation is no valid excuse

Whoever is doing the delegating, is responsible for the information to be collected, organized and made accessible and retrievable at any possible time where that could be useful.

In other words, that "plausible deniability" is not possible.

3.4 A lack of resources is no valid excuse

If there are not enough resources, like time or money, ask for help, broadcast your need for assistance.

If sufficient resources can't be mobilized, the problem actually isn't that important to people. See 1.3.2.

If there are too many different topics in a schedule to get any single thing done, the issues need to be coordinated with other people.

The amount of work necessary to deliver good information is fixed. There isn't necessarily a problem if somehting is work in progress, there is a problem when "work in progress" is used as an excuse to actually deliver any usable information.

4. manipulation and interconnection

manipulation is real

Being pedantic about semantics isn't the goal, the goal is to deliver good information.

Distraction, diversion, delaying, misrepresenation and faking information are all common practice. It is not fair to outright accuse people of it, in a "guilty until proven innocent" way, but "innocent until proven guilty" is too weak in practice as well.

The point is, the "accusation" has to be possible and socially acceptable. The demand for good information, should not be met deflection, delegation ad hominem or "do it yourself then" responses.

Asking for sources and proof is the self defense mechanism against manipulation and is in the interest of society in general. Respect it.

4.2 independence is hard

Independence is hard to achieve, given real world limitations.

Individuals can be intertwined with their subjects, through no fault of their own. As such, journalists and researchers who have specialized in certain areas shouldn't be thought of as independent from their source of income or funding, or from frequented sources.

In other words, a journalist who has specialized in interviewing politicians, isn't independent of the subject. A scientist who has built a career out of his subject being interesting and relevant, shouldn't be trusted with evaluating whether that continues to be the case.

Public broadcasting institutions shouldn't be thought of as entirely independent of the states that are financing them. There are hidden incentives at play that make objective separation impossible.

These factors are the reason we want multiple sources that are independent from each other, to be able to confirm the information and perspective isn't influenced.

5. known Issues and warnings

5.1 the ideal situation is not realistic

It should be noyed that the ideal situation in 0. is obviously not realistic but is still used as a mental model by nearly everyone.

Meaning, just because a topic is well know, well understood and recognized does not mean action will be taken. Everyone still expects it to happen anyway. It does not.

If you are about to take a large risk, please make sure you're not relying on 0. to work.

5.2 creation and application

I think it should be the responsibility of journalism and science to provide better information. I don't think this is happening right now.

I think it should be the responsibility of politics to act on good information and to control the quality of the information provided to them.

5.3 It's everywhere

I have not seen a organization that actually takes their feedback process seriously. Democracy, companies, social constructs.

x. I don't want a witch hunt, I want good information

I'm not calling for resignation or apologies or law suits or punishments.

I can't deny I would find them enjoyable, but that's not the point. It's important to stay focused and the focus is on good information, not personal satisfaction.
