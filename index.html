Disclaimer

All arguments presented are nothing but my opinion and my perspective. See Core 7 f. I am from Germany, which naturally carries some bias.

I am retelling things from my perspective, which is a single point of view, but it would be impossible for the issues to come up if they weren't "normalized". Our society is too interconnected for significant outliers or problems with *known* solutions to remain unnoticed.

As an example, let's take the case of a "dark patterns" in social media. It is known that endlessly scrolling content lists are supposed to "engage" users, because that's in the interest of the company operating the network. Whether it is in the interest of the user is of no concern.

If such behavior was unusually bad, and only one company was doing it, it would attract attention and hopefully someone would step up to fix the issue.

But that doesn't happen. It's not rationally evaluated whether a situation or behavior is a problem, instead if everyone is doing it, it is largely ignored.
