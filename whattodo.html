Finding out what to do.

The basic principle of how we deal with things in this world is by comparing an abstract ideal, theory or goal and what's actually the case.

There are a number of challenges to this that I want to adress. In most cases I can see, the biggest problem is bad communication. People misunderstand, aren't precise or get sidetracked. So I aim to collect and organize some tools and easy directions and simple arguments to follow, to adress the most common errors.

The split between abstract and real is easy and that's a big advantage. For example:

TheoryReal
Traffic rules are designed for safety,
drivers take tests.		Accidents happen anyway.

When talking about how to avoid another accident, we can approach from two sides:

  • something with the rules or the testing is wrong
  • there was something else about the real situation that we need to avoid

The best traffic rules won't help if a driver has a heart attack, and the best, healthiest drivers will cause accidents if there is something wrong with the situation created by the rules.
