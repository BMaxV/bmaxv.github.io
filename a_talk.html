1.05 W-questions

W questions are *the* most basic, most easy to agree on set of questions around events, I learned about them in school, they are supposed to be the bread and butter of journalism.

But they have universal relevance.

The example I want to use through this section, is organizing a party. If I want to organize a party, it is necessary that my guests be informed Where, and When, it will take place, that they are invitited to a party, which covers the what, and that they are invited takes care of the "who". "why" is optional, it can be a birthdayparty, or a costume party. This can slightly affect the proceedings.

If any of these details are missed, there is the chance, that the party won't go smoothly. If they don't know where to go or when the party will take place, they might miss it. If I forget to invite them, they can't come. That it is a party, and not some other, perhaps mandatory event, sets theme, background, gives the option to weigh coming or declining, etc..

1.06 risk management

Risk management is about dealing with uncertainty. For example I might plan to buy exactly 69 beers for my party. However, there is a chance that the store will not have 69 beers. In case they don't, I will have to get beer from a different place, to get to 69.

Known as "murphys law" "Anything that can go wrong, will go wrong." is the mantra that you should know your risks and perpare measures in case they come to pass.

Insurance is one of the great achievements of civilized society. The idea is that while a horrible tragedy might befall one of us, it won't happen to everyone at the same time. So we each pitch in, and give the collected money to the poor victim. Because of empathy. At the same time, it creates a shared interest to avoid unnecessary danger, because if we can minimized accidents overall, we have to pay less.

We're calculating it like this:

chance of the event * value of the damage = expected value

Where the chance of the event is usually the number of actual events, between everyone participating over some time.

Let's say we're 200 people, 10 of us get sick, and need medicine worth 5€ each. the chance to get sick would be 10/200 = 1/20 or 5%.

The value of the damage or cost would be 10* 5€, = 50€, but because we're all pitching in, the shared cost would only amount to 50*1/20 or 2,50€.

Combined with planning this is known as "strategy", we're planning and taking into account possible failures and thinking of ways to deal with those failures

1.05 priorities

Priorities are a tool for decision making. You should think of them as a list of important things, sorted by importance and indexed.

For example taking my party, sending invitations would be more important than picking what color the table cloth should be. I can have a party with any kind of table cloth, but I can't have a party without people, therefore people are more important than the color of my table cloth. So I write down people first and the color of my table cloth somewhere below that.

whenever I think of something new to consider for my party, I can find a place somewhere on my list. Drinks are more important than the color of the tablecloth as well.

When I am lost or confused what needs to be done, what I should spend time on organizing and doing, all I need to do, is check my list and go down from the top.
